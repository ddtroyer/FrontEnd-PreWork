About Me

My career path has covered a lot of different areas, which at first don't seem to be connected. But each has contributed something towards how I became a developer.

I spent several years working for The Commercial Record, a weekly newspaper in Saugatuck, Michigan. I started out as a typist and eventually became a copy editor and journalist. I also assisted with laying out the articles and advertisements. I have a good understanding of layouts, fonts, kerning, and how to manipulate objects so they'll fit in whatever space is available.

My next position was as a contractor at Palisades Nuclear Plant near Covert, Michigan. I worked in several different departments, mostly in document control. I learned a lot about databases as well as the importance of documentation and attention to detail.

I spent two years in California working at Xilinx in the legal department. In addition to document control, I also learned about the different parts of a legal contract and eventually was able to negotiate non-disclosure agreements. This position reinforced skills learned previously but also showed me that I was capable of learning anything.

My time in California was an adventure but I missed seeing the seasons change, thunderstorms, the lakes, and, most of all, my friends and family.

In the background this whole time has been an interest in computers, not just as a user but as someone who is fascinated by how they work and what can be accomplished with them. After several years of sporadically taking online tutorials, I jumped into the programming world by enrolling in Grand Circus' inaugurial C#/.NET bootcamp in Grand Rapids. Having completed that program, I wanted more. I applied to Grand Circus' Front-End bootcamp in order to become a full-stack developer.
